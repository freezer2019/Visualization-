The impact of COVID-19 in the United States

A review of COVID-19 statistics and projecting future trends

Understanding the spread of COVID-19 is a critical task to mitigate the virus. The global spread of the virus has overwhelmed health systems, and caused widespread social and economic disruption. What we know so far is there are multiple social and environmental factors influencing the spread. Our focus was to predict the number of ventilators needed as Coronavirus cases rise. Data was sourced from the COVID Tracking Project and CDC for training and testing the models. All our data comes from state and territory public health authorities or official statements from state officials and included daily positive cases, hospitalizations, deaths, and number of patients on ventilators. After cleaning the data, we tested and evaluated different machine learning models, including linear regression, random forest, and neural networks.

The New York state data was sourced from the COVID Tracking Project. The data was filtered to include a small subset for testing and analysis. The dates range from September until December. A linear regression model was applied to train and test a small number of variables: positive cases, currently hospitalised, number of patients currently in ICU and patients currently on ventilator.

The US national data was sourced the COVID Tracking Project. A linear regression model was applied to train and test a number of variables: positive cases, the increase in positive cases, currently hospitalised, number of patients currently in ICU, recovered patients and patients currently on ventilator.
